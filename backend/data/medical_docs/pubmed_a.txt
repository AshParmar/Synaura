Title: Linear (Discoid) Atelectasis on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- Thin **linear or plate-like opacity** (horizontal or oblique)
- Usually located in **lower lung zones**
- Minimal volume loss
- No significant mediastinal shift
- Often parallel to diaphragm

Causes:
- Shallow breathing (postoperative, pain)
- Hypoventilation
- Mucus plugging (minor airway obstruction)

Symptoms:
- Often asymptomatic
- Mild dyspnea in some cases

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing characteristic linear opacity
- Clinical context (post-surgery, immobility)
- CT rarely required

Clinical Importance:
- Very common, especially in hospitalized patients
- Usually reversible with physiotherapy and deep breathing
- Important not to confuse with fibrosis or consolidation

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pulmonary fibrosis (persistent, irregular lines)
- Subsegmental pneumonia
- Scarring
