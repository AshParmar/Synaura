Title: Pericardial Effusion Mimicking Cardiomegaly on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- Globular, “water-bottle” shaped cardiac silhouette
- Smooth, symmetric enlargement of heart borders
- Rapid increase in cardiac size on serial X-rays
- Clear lung fields (unless associated condition)
- No chamber-specific contour changes

Causes:
- Fluid accumulation in pericardial sac
- Infection (viral, tuberculosis)
- Malignancy
- Uremia or autoimmune disease

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea
- Chest discomfort
- In severe cases: signs of cardiac tamponade

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing globular cardiomegaly
- Echocardiography confirming pericardial fluid
- Clinical context (acute vs chronic onset)

Clinical Importance:
- Can mimic true cardiomegaly → critical to differentiate
- Risk of cardiac tamponade (life-threatening)
- Requires urgent management in severe cases

Differential Diagnosis:
- True cardiomegaly (chamber enlargement)
- Dilated cardiomyopathy
- Mediastinal mass
