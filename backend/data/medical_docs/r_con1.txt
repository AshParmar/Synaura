The texts distinguish between lobar pneumonia, typically caused by bacterial infections like Streptococcus pneumoniae, and other forms such as lipoid pneumonia resulting from oil inhalation. Diagnostic tools are discussed in detail, highlighting how chest X-rays, CT scans, and ultrasounds identify characteristic signs like "air bronchograms" or specific density variations. Furthermore, the literature compares pneumonia with pulmonary edema, noting that while both cause breathing difficulties, the former is infectious whereas the latter often stems from heart failure. Management strategies focus on pathogen-specific treatments, such as antibiotics, alongside preventative measures like vaccination and smoking cessation.
