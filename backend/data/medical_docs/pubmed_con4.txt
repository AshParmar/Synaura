Title: Aspiration Pneumonia Presenting as Dependent Lung Consolidation

Radiological Findings:
- Consolidation in **dependent lung regions**:
  - Lower lobes (upright patients)
  - Posterior upper lobes or superior lower lobes (supine patients)
- Patchy or confluent air-space opacities
- Ill-defined segmental consolidation
- Possible air bronchograms
- In severe cases: cavitation or abscess formation

Causes:
- Aspiration of gastric contents or oropharyngeal secretions
- Impaired swallowing or consciousness
- Neurological disorders or sedation

Symptoms:
- Cough
- Fever
- Difficulty breathing
- History of aspiration event (important clue)

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing consolidation in dependent areas
- CT for detailed localization and complications
- Clinical history of aspiration risk
- Microbiological evaluation if severe

Clinical Importance:
- Common but often under-recognized cause of pneumonia
- Location of consolidation is key diagnostic clue
- Requires specific management (antibiotics + aspiration prevention)
- Risk of complications like lung abscess if untreated

Differential Diagnosis:
- Lobar pneumonia (not dependent distribution)
- Pulmonary edema (bilateral symmetric pattern)
- Atelectasis (volume loss present)
- Lung abscess (if cavitation dominates)
