Title: Atelectasis on Chest X-ray: Collapse of Lung Parenchyma

Radiological Findings:
- Key feature: **Volume loss in lung**
- Increased opacity in affected region (collapsed lung)
- **Displacement of fissures** toward area of collapse
- **Mediastinal shift toward affected side**
- Elevation of hemidiaphragm
- Crowding of ribs and vascular markings
- Compensatory overinflation of adjacent lung
- Linear or wedge-shaped opacity (depending on lobe)

Causes:
- Airway obstruction (e.g., mucus plug, tumor)
- External compression (pleural effusion, mass, pneumothorax)
- Increased alveolar surface tension (surfactant loss)

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea
- Reduced breath sounds over affected area

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray (PA + lateral views essential)
- Identification of volume loss + structural shifts
- CT scan if cause is unclear or to detect obstruction
- Clinical correlation with underlying condition

Clinical Importance:
- Indicates underlying pathology (not a primary disease)
- Differentiation from consolidation is critical:
  - Atelectasis → volume loss
  - Consolidation → no volume loss
- Early recognition allows reversible management
- Untreated cases may lead to infection or hypoxia

Differential Diagnosis:
- Lobar pneumonia (no volume loss, air bronchograms)
- Pleural effusion (mass effect, shifts away)
- Lung mass (localized opacity without volume loss)
- Pulmonary fibrosis (chronic volume loss with distortion)
