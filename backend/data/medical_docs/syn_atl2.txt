Title: Compressive Atelectasis due to Pleural Effusion

Radiological Findings:
- Adjacent lung collapse near pleural fluid
- Curvilinear opacity along effusion margin
- Reduced lung volume near compressed region
- Effusion signs:
  - Meniscus sign
  - Blunted costophrenic angle
- Mediastinal shift **away from effusion** (if large)

Causes:
- Pleural effusion
- Pneumothorax (tension)
- Intrathoracic mass

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea
- Chest discomfort

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing effusion + adjacent collapse
- Ultrasound to confirm fluid
- CT for detailed assessment

Clinical Importance:
- Collapse is **secondary to external compression**
- Treating underlying cause (e.g., draining effusion) reverses collapse
- Important in ICU and emergency settings

Differential Diagnosis:
- Primary atelectasis (shift toward side)
- Pneumonia (no compression pattern)
- Lung collapse from obstruction
