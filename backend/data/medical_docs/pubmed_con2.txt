Title: Bronchopneumonia with Multifocal Patchy Consolidation

Radiological Findings:
- Patchy, poorly defined consolidations
- Peribronchial and multilobar distribution
- Bilateral scattered opacities
- Centrilobular nodular opacities may be present
- No clear lobar boundaries

Causes:
- Staphylococcus aureus
- Gram-negative bacteria (hospital-acquired infections)
- Aspiration-related infections

Symptoms:
- Fever
- Cough
- Dyspnea

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing patchy multifocal consolidation
- CT for better visualization of distribution
- Clinical context (hospital setting, aspiration risk)

Clinical Importance:
- Common in hospitalized or immunocompromised patients
- Indicates more diffuse infection → may require aggressive treatment
- Helps differentiate from localized lobar pneumonia

Differential Diagnosis:
- Viral pneumonia (interstitial pattern dominant)
- ARDS (diffuse bilateral opacities)
- Pulmonary edema
