Title: Pulmonary Edema Mimicking Consolidation on Imaging

Radiological Findings:
- Bilateral, symmetric air-space opacities
- Perihilar “bat-wing” pattern of consolidation
- Kerley B lines (interstitial edema)
- Associated pleural effusions
- Rapidly changing opacities on serial imaging

Causes:
- Congestive heart failure (most common)
- Fluid overload
- Renal failure

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Orthopnea
- Reduced exercise tolerance

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing bilateral perihilar consolidation pattern
- Clinical signs of cardiac dysfunction
- Response to diuretics supports diagnosis

Clinical Importance:
- Can mimic pneumonia → risk of misdiagnosis
- Requires cardiac management, not antibiotics
- Rapidly reversible with proper treatment

Differential Diagnosis:
- Bilateral pneumonia
- ARDS
- Pulmonary hemorrhage
