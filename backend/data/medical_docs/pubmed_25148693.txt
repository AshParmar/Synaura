Title: Host Immune Response and Tissue Resilience in Pneumonia Progression

Radiological Findings:
- Early stage:
  - Subtle or mild ground-glass opacities
  - Minimal interstitial changes
- Progressive infection:
  - Patchy or diffuse consolidation
  - Interstitial thickening due to inflammatory response
- Severe cases:
  - Bilateral diffuse opacities (extensive lung involvement)
  - Features resembling ARDS (ground-glass + consolidation)
- Resolution phase:
  - Gradual clearing of opacities
  - Possible residual interstitial markings

Causes:
- Microbial infection (bacterial, viral, atypical pathogens)
- Host immune response (innate + adaptive)
- Imbalance between:
  - Resistance (pathogen clearance)
  - Resilience (tissue protection from damage)

Symptoms:
- Fever
- Cough
- Dyspnea (in severe cases)

Diagnosis:
- Imaging (X-ray/CT) showing progression from mild to severe lung involvement
- Clinical assessment of infection severity
- Lab markers of inflammation and immune response
- Consider patient age and immune status

Clinical Importance:
- Disease severity depends not only on pathogen but also host response
- Excess immune activation → lung damage and severe pneumonia
- Reduced resilience → higher risk of complications and ARDS
- Age-related immune changes influence pneumonia outcomes
- Helps in designing therapies targeting immune modulation, not just infection

Differential Diagnosis:
- Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)
- Viral pneumonia with diffuse lung injury
- Interstitial lung diseases
- Pulmonary edema (non-infectious cause of diffuse opacities)
