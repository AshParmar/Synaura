Title: Lobar Atelectasis with Fissure Displacement

Radiological Findings:
- Dense opacity involving a lung lobe
- **Displacement of interlobar fissures** toward collapse
- **Mediastinal shift toward affected side**
- Loss of normal lobe volume
- Compensatory hyperinflation of adjacent lobes
- Possible air bronchograms (crowded)

Causes:
- Bronchial obstruction (tumor, mucus plug)
- Foreign body aspiration

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea
- Reduced breath sounds

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing lobar opacity + volume loss
- CT scan to identify obstructing lesion
- Bronchoscopy if obstruction suspected

Clinical Importance:
- Strongly suggests **airway obstruction**
- Requires urgent evaluation to rule out malignancy
- Differentiation from lobar pneumonia is critical

Differential Diagnosis:
- Lobar pneumonia (no fissure displacement)
- Pleural effusion
- Lung mass
