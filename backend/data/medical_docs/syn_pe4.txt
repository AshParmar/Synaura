Title: Loculated Pleural Effusion on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- **Non-dependent, localized opacity** along pleura
- Lenticular or biconvex shape
- Does not shift with patient position
- May mimic mass or consolidation
- Sharp margins against lung parenchyma

Causes:
- Complicated parapneumonic effusion
- Empyema
- Post-inflammatory adhesions

Symptoms:
- Fever (if infectious)
- Chest pain
- Dyspnea

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing atypical fixed pleural opacity
- Ultrasound showing septations and loculations
- CT for detailed assessment

Clinical Importance:
- Suggests **complicated effusion → requires drainage**
- Important to differentiate from tumor
- Delayed treatment can lead to empyema

Differential Diagnosis:
- Lung abscess
- Pleural tumor
- Consolidation (follows lung anatomy, not pleural shape)
