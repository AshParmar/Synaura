Title: Cardiomegaly with Pulmonary Congestion in Heart Failure

Radiological Findings:
- Enlarged cardiac silhouette
- Pulmonary venous congestion:
  - Prominent upper lobe vessels
- Interstitial edema:
  - Kerley B lines
- Alveolar edema:
  - Bilateral perihilar “bat-wing” opacities
- Possible pleural effusions

Causes:
- Left-sided heart failure
- Ischemic heart disease
- Chronic hypertension
- Valvular dysfunction

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Orthopnea
- Peripheral edema

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing cardiomegaly + pulmonary edema pattern
- Echocardiography to assess cardiac function
- Clinical signs of heart failure

Clinical Importance:
- Radiology helps assess severity of heart failure
- Guides urgent treatment (diuretics, oxygen)
- Monitoring response via serial imaging

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pulmonary edema without cardiomegaly (ARDS)
- Bilateral pneumonia
- Renal failure-related fluid overload
