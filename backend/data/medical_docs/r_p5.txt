Mycoplasma pneumoniae in pediatric respiratory infections: A comprehensive review "Mycoplasma pneumoniae is a key contributor to community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) in children, particularly those between 5 and 15 years of age. Its clinical presentation is often atypical, complicating timely diagnosis. Due to the absence of a cell wall, M. pneumoniae is naturally resistant to β-lactam antibiotics, necessitating treatment with macrolides. The disease may manifest as mild upper respiratory symptoms or progress to more severe lower respiratory tract infections, occasionally involving other systems such as the central nervous, dermatological, and cardiac systems. Accurate identification typically requires both clinical assessment and diagnostic tools like polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and serological testing. The growing incidence of macrolide-resistant strains worldwide poses a serious concern. This review aims to explore the pathogen’s distribution, mechanisms of disease, clinical features, diagnostic options, therapeutic approaches, and possible complications in the pediatric population."
