Title: Small Pleural Effusion Detection on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- Early **blunting of costophrenic angle** (most sensitive sign)
- Subtle loss of sharp diaphragm outline
- Minimal basal opacity
- Best seen on **lateral view** (posterior costophrenic recess)
- May be missed on standard PA view if very small

Causes:
- Early heart failure
- Mild parapneumonic effusion
- Post-surgical or inflammatory states

Symptoms:
- Often asymptomatic
- Mild dyspnea (if progressive)

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray (PA + lateral views)
- Ultrasound for confirmation of small fluid volume
- Clinical correlation for underlying cause

Clinical Importance:
- Early detection prevents progression
- Easily overlooked → requires careful evaluation
- Important in follow-up imaging

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pleural thickening
- Subpulmonic effusion
- Basal atelectasis
