Title: Cardiomegaly due to High-Output State in Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (HHT)

Radiological Findings:
- Chest X-ray:
  - Enlarged cardiac silhouette (cardiomegaly)
  - No focal lung consolidation or primary pulmonary cause
- Echocardiography:
  - Dilatation of both ventricles
  - Features of **high cardiac output state**
  - No valvular or structural cardiac disease
- Abdominal imaging (US/CT/MRI):
  - Enlarged hepatic vasculature
  - Multiple **arteriovenous shunts in liver**
  - Angiomatous transformation of liver

Causes:
- Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (HHT)
- Systemic arteriovenous malformations (especially hepatic)
- High-output cardiac state due to vascular shunting

Symptoms:
- May include fatigue, dyspnea
- Signs of systemic vascular disease (e.g., telangiectasias, bleeding)

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing cardiomegaly
- Echocardiography excluding primary cardiac disease
- CT/MRI confirming hepatic vascular malformations
- Genetic testing (ACVRL1 mutation)
- Clinical correlation with cutaneous and mucosal lesions

Clinical Importance:
- Cardiomegaly may be **secondary to systemic vascular disease**, not primary heart pathology
- Identifying high-output state prevents misdiagnosis as cardiomyopathy
- Important for detecting HHT → risk of multi-organ involvement
- Guides management toward treating underlying vascular disorder

Differential Diagnosis:
- Primary cardiomyopathy
- Valvular heart disease
- Pericardial effusion
- Other causes of high-output heart failure (e.g., anemia, thyroid disease)
