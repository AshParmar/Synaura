Title: Obstructive (Resorption) Atelectasis from Airway Blockage

Radiological Findings:
- Segmental or lobar opacity
- Marked **volume loss**
- **Mediastinal shift toward affected side**
- Crowded vascular markings
- Hyperinflation of remaining lung
- Possible absence of air bronchograms (complete obstruction)

Causes:
- Mucus plug
- Endobronchial tumor
- Foreign body obstruction

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea
- Sudden onset in acute obstruction

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing collapse with volume loss
- CT scan to locate obstruction
- Bronchoscopy for confirmation and treatment

Clinical Importance:
- Common cause of significant atelectasis
- May indicate serious underlying disease (e.g., tumor)
- Reversible if obstruction is removed early

Differential Diagnosis:
- Lobar pneumonia (air bronchograms present, no volume loss)
- Pleural effusion (pushes structures away)
- Pulmonary fibrosis (chronic changes)
