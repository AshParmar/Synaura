Title: Chlamydophila pneumoniae in Pediatric Community-Acquired Pneumonia

Radiological Findings:
- Predominant interstitial pattern on chest X-ray
- Diffuse or patchy reticulonodular opacities
- Perihilar infiltrates (bilateral or unilateral)
- Focal consolidation in some cases
- Overall atypical pneumonia appearance rather than lobar pattern

Causes:
- Infection with Chlamydophila pneumoniae (atypical intracellular bacterium)
- Frequent co-infection with Mycoplasma pneumoniae
- Higher susceptibility in children with underlying diseases

Symptoms:
- Persistent cough (key symptom)
- Fever
- Gradual onset of respiratory illness

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing interstitial/atypical pneumonia pattern
- PCR-based detection of bacterial DNA
- Serology (IgM/IgG) as supportive evidence
- Clinical correlation with prolonged cough and mild systemic features

Clinical Importance:
- Atypical CAP → does not respond to standard beta-lactam antibiotics
- Requires macrolide-based therapy
- Early identification reduces complications
- Important in pediatric patients with comorbidities

Differential Diagnosis:
- Mycoplasma pneumoniae (very similar interstitial pattern)
- Viral pneumonia (diffuse interstitial opacities)
- Typical bacterial pneumonia (lobar consolidation dominant)
- Other atypical pneumonias (e.g., Legionella)
