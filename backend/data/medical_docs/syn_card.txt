Title: Right Heart Enlargement and Cardiomegaly in Pulmonary Hypertension

Radiological Findings:
- Enlarged cardiac silhouette with right-sided predominance
- Prominent right heart border
- Enlargement of pulmonary arteries
- Pruning of peripheral pulmonary vessels
- Elevated cardiac apex (right ventricular enlargement)

Causes:
- Pulmonary hypertension
- Chronic lung disease (COPD, fibrosis)
- Pulmonary embolism
- Congenital heart disease

Symptoms:
- Dyspnea on exertion
- Fatigue
- Signs of right heart failure

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing right-sided cardiomegaly and vascular changes
- Echocardiography estimating pulmonary pressures
- CT or further workup for underlying cause

Clinical Importance:
- Suggests chronic pulmonary vascular disease
- Helps differentiate right vs left heart pathology
- Important for early detection of pulmonary hypertension

Differential Diagnosis:
- Left heart failure (different radiologic pattern)
- Pericardial effusion
- Congenital cardiac anomalies
