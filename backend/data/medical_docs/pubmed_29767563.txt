Title: Systemic Immune and Organ-Level Response in Pneumonia

Radiological Findings:
- Early:
  - Mild ground-glass opacities
  - Subtle interstitial changes
- Progressive:
  - Patchy or lobar consolidation
  - Interstitial thickening from inflammation
- Severe / systemic involvement:
  - Bilateral diffuse opacities
  - Ground-glass + consolidation (lung injury pattern)
- Chronic / post-infection:
  - Residual fibrosis-like interstitial markings
  - Persistent lung abnormalities in severe cases

Causes:
- Microbial infection (bacterial, viral, atypical)
- Dysregulated immune response across organs
- Excessive inflammation causing tissue injury
- Contribution from extrapulmonary organs (liver, bone marrow, immune system signaling)

Symptoms:
- Cough
- Fever
- Dyspnea (in moderate to severe cases)

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray / CT showing progression from interstitial changes to consolidation
- Clinical evaluation of systemic involvement
- Lab indicators of inflammation and immune activation
- Assessment of comorbidities and organ involvement

Clinical Importance:
- Pneumonia is a systemic disease, not just lung infection
- Interorgan signaling affects severity and recovery
- Excess inflammation can lead to acute lung injury and long-term complications
- Can trigger chronic pulmonary disease and extrapulmonary sequelae
- Important for designing treatments targeting both infection and immune regulation

Differential Diagnosis:
- Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)
- Chronic interstitial lung disease
- Pulmonary edema (cardiac or systemic origin)
- Sepsis-related lung injury
