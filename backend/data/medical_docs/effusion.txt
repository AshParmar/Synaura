Pleural effusion is the accumulation of fluid in the pleural space surrounding the lungs.

Radiological Findings:
On chest X-rays, pleural effusion appears as blunting of the costophrenic angle or fluid layering at the lung base. 
Large effusions may obscure lung fields.

Symptoms:
Symptoms include shortness of breath, chest discomfort, and reduced lung expansion.

Treatment:
Treatment depends on the cause and may include drainage (thoracentesis), medications, or treating underlying conditions.

Differential Diagnosis:
Effusion can be confused with consolidation, pneumonia, or lung collapse in imaging.
