Title: Subpulmonic Pleural Effusion on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- Apparent **elevated hemidiaphragm**
- Lateral displacement of diaphragm peak
- Increased distance between gastric bubble and diaphragm (left side)
- Blunted costophrenic angle may be absent initially
- Pseudo-diaphragm contour

Causes:
- Early pleural effusion collecting beneath lung
- Heart failure
- Postoperative or inflammatory states

Symptoms:
- Mild dyspnea
- Often subtle or asymptomatic

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray with suspicious diaphragm elevation
- Lateral decubitus view or ultrasound for confirmation
- CT if unclear

Clinical Importance:
- Commonly misdiagnosed as diaphragm elevation
- Early recognition prevents missed effusion
- Important in subtle or early-stage disease

Differential Diagnosis:
- Diaphragmatic paralysis
- Subphrenic abscess
- Basal atelectasis
