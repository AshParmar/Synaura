Consolidation refers to the filling of lung airspaces with fluid, pus, blood, or cells, leading to increased density on imaging.

Radiological Findings:
Consolidation appears as a homogeneous area of increased opacity on chest X-ray. 
Air bronchograms may be visible, where air-filled bronchi are seen within the dense region.

Symptoms:
Patients may present with fever, productive cough, chest pain, and shortness of breath.

Causes:
Common causes include pneumonia, lung infections, and inflammatory conditions.

Treatment:
Treatment depends on the underlying cause and often includes antibiotics if infection is present.

Differential Diagnosis:
Consolidation can be confused with atelectasis, pulmonary edema, or lung masses.
