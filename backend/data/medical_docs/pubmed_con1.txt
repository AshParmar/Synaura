Title: Lobar Pneumonia Presenting as Dense Pulmonary Consolidation

Radiological Findings:
- Dense, homogeneous consolidation involving a single lobe
- Presence of air bronchograms within consolidated region
- Sharp anatomical boundaries (limited by fissures)
- Silhouette sign (loss of heart/diaphragm borders depending on lobe)
- No significant volume loss (distinguishes from atelectasis)

Causes:
- Streptococcus pneumoniae (most common)
- Other typical bacterial pathogens

Symptoms:
- High fever
- Productive cough
- Chest pain

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing lobar consolidation with air bronchograms
- Clinical features of acute infection
- Lab confirmation if required

Clinical Importance:
- Classic pattern of bacterial pneumonia → guides antibiotic therapy
- Rapid identification enables early treatment
- Helps differentiate from atypical pneumonia

Differential Diagnosis:
- Atelectasis (with volume loss)
- Lung abscess (cavitation)
- Pulmonary infarction
