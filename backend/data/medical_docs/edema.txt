Pulmonary edema is the accumulation of fluid within the lung interstitium and alveolar spaces.

Radiological Findings:
On chest X-ray, pulmonary edema appears as diffuse bilateral opacities, often described as a "bat-wing" pattern. 
There may also be Kerley B lines and vascular congestion.

Symptoms:
Common symptoms include severe shortness of breath, rapid breathing, and reduced oxygen levels.

Causes:
Pulmonary edema is often caused by heart failure (cardiogenic edema) but may also occur due to lung injury or infection.

Treatment:
Treatment includes oxygen therapy, diuretics, and management of the underlying cause such as heart failure.

Differential Diagnosis:
Pulmonary edema may resemble pneumonia or acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) on imaging.
