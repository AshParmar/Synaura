Title: Massive Pleural Effusion with Mediastinal Shift

Radiological Findings:
- Complete or near-complete **hemithorax opacification**
- **Contralateral mediastinal shift** (trachea pushed away)
- Loss of diaphragm and cardiac border on affected side
- No air bronchograms (distinguishes from consolidation)

Causes:
- Malignancy
- Massive parapneumonic effusion
- Hemothorax

Symptoms:
- Severe dyspnea
- Chest heaviness

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing white-out lung with mediastinal shift
- Ultrasound to confirm fluid nature
- CT for cause identification

Clinical Importance:
- Indicates large fluid accumulation → urgent drainage may be needed
- Mediastinal shift suggests significant pressure effect
- Helps differentiate from lung collapse (which shifts toward lesion)

Differential Diagnosis:
- Total lung collapse (shift toward side)
- Massive consolidation (air bronchograms present)
- Large tumor occupying hemithorax
