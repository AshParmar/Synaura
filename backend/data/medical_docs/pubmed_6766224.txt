Title: Rounded Atelectasis Mimicking Pulmonary Mass on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- **Rounded or oval mass-like opacity** (typically 2–5 cm)
- Located **peripherally**, abutting pleura (pleural-based)
- Commonly in **posterior lower lobes**
- **“Comet-tail sign”**:
  - Curved bronchovascular structures converging toward lesion
- Associated **pleural thickening** adjacent to lesion
- No true invasive features (unlike malignancy)

Causes:
- Chronic pleural disease (e.g., prior inflammation, asbestos exposure)
- Pleural thickening leading to folding/collapse of adjacent lung

Symptoms:
- Usually asymptomatic
- Often incidentally detected

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing pleural-based rounded opacity
- CT scan confirms:
  - Comet-tail sign
  - Pleural thickening
- Stability over time supports benign nature

Clinical Importance:
- Frequently **misdiagnosed as lung tumor**
- Recognition avoids unnecessary invasive procedures (e.g., thoracotomy)
- Represents benign, non-progressive condition

Differential Diagnosis:
- Primary lung malignancy
- Metastatic pulmonary nodule
- Pulmonary abscess
- Loculated pleural effusion
