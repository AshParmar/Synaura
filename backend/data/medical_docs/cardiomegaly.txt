Cardiomegaly refers to an enlarged heart and is often associated with cardiovascular disease.

Radiological Findings:
On chest X-ray, cardiomegaly is indicated by an increased cardiothoracic ratio, where the heart appears enlarged relative to the chest.

Symptoms:
Patients may experience fatigue, shortness of breath, and swelling in the legs.

Treatment:
Management includes treating underlying causes such as hypertension or heart failure.

Differential Diagnosis:
Cardiomegaly should be distinguished from pericardial effusion and other conditions that enlarge the cardiac silhouette.
