Title: Left Ventricular Enlargement and Cardiomegaly on Chest X-ray

Radiological Findings:
- Increased cardiothoracic ratio (CTR > 0.5 on PA view)
- Enlarged cardiac silhouette
- Left ventricular enlargement signs:
  - Left heart border displaced **leftward and inferiorly**
  - **Rounded cardiac apex** (key feature)
  - Inferior extension of cardiac shadow
- On lateral view:
  - Increased posterior cardiac shadow
- Important pitfall:
  - AP projection may falsely exaggerate heart size (magnification effect)

Causes:
- Chronic hypertension
- Aortic valve disease
- Cardiomyopathy
- Volume or pressure overload of left ventricle

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Exercise intolerance

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray (PA view preferred) for CTR assessment
- Identification of left ventricular contour changes
- Echocardiography for confirmation and functional assessment
- Clinical correlation with cardiovascular findings

Clinical Importance:
- Left ventricular enlargement is a key indicator of chronic cardiac stress
- Early detection helps prevent progression to heart failure
- Differentiating true enlargement from projection artifacts is critical
- Guides further cardiac evaluation and management

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pericardial effusion (globular enlarged silhouette)
- AP film magnification (false cardiomegaly)
- Right ventricular enlargement (different contour changes)
- Mediastinal widening or masses
