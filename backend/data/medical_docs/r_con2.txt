Hypodense consolidation - SciELO" This paper presents a case report and discusses the differential diagnosis of pulmonary consolidation based on Hounsfield unit (HU) densities
. It details the case of a 72-year-old male with advanced Alzheimer's disease who presented with chronic cough and dyspnea, and whose chest CT revealed bilateral lower lobe consolidations
. The consolidations exhibited negative density values (-13 to -86 HU), which is highly suggestive of intrapulmonary fat
. The patient was ultimately diagnosed with exogenous lipoid pneumonia caused by the chronic aspiration of mineral oil used to treat severe constipation
