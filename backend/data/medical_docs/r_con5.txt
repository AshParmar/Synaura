Lobar pneumonia - Wikipedia" This encyclopedia entry defines lobar pneumonia as a specific form of pneumonia where an inflammatory exudate causes consolidation across a large, continuous area of a lung lobe
. The pathophysiology is categorized into four classic stages: congestion, red hepatization (consolidation with red blood cells and neutrophils), grey hepatization (disintegration of red blood cells), and resolution
. It notes that Streptococcus pneumoniae, Haemophilus influenzae, and Moraxella catarrhalis are the most common causative organisms
. The article also explains that children typically develop "round pneumonia" instead of lobar pneumonia due to underdeveloped pores of Kohn
