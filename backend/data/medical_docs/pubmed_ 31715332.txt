Title: Pulmonary Consolidation on Chest X-ray: Imaging Features and AI-Based Detection

Radiological Findings:
- Key feature: **Increased lung opacity (consolidation)**
- Homogeneous or patchy **air-space opacification**
- **Air bronchograms** may be visible within dense regions
- Loss of normal lung markings in affected area
- Lobar, segmental, or multifocal distribution
- Blurring of adjacent structures (e.g., heart border, diaphragm)
- Causes of opacity content:
  - Pus (infection)
  - Fluid (edema)
  - Blood (hemorrhage)
  - Proteinaceous material

Causes:
- Bacterial pneumonia (most common)
- Viral or atypical infections
- Pulmonary edema
- Hemorrhage
- Inflammatory or protein deposition disorders

Symptoms:
- Cough
- Fever
- Shortness of breath

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray identifying consolidation pattern
- AI models (e.g., CNNs) assist in detecting and localizing opacities
- Clinical correlation to determine underlying cause
- Further imaging (CT) if unclear or complicated

Clinical Importance:
- Consolidation is a **core radiological sign** in lung disease
- Early and accurate detection improves diagnosis and treatment
- AI enhances radiologist performance and reduces missed findings
- Helps in triage, especially in high-volume or resource-limited settings

Differential Diagnosis:
- Atelectasis (volume loss with opacity)
- Pulmonary edema (bilateral diffuse opacities)
- Lung malignancy (mass-like consolidation)
- ARDS (diffuse bilateral involvement)
