Pulmonary consolidation - Wikipedia" This encyclopedia entry defines pulmonary consolidation as a condition where normally compressible, aerated lung tissue is filled with liquid such as edema, inflammatory exudate, pus, or blood
. Clinical signs include reduced thoracic expansion, increased vocal fremitus, bronchial breath sounds, and increased vocal resonance
. Radiologically, consolidated tissue appears as a radio-opaque (white) area on standard X-rays and CT scans, and its presence is required for a clinical diagnosis of pneumonia
