Pneumonia is a lung infection that appears on chest X-rays as areas of opacity or consolidation. 
It is commonly observed in the lower lung zones.

Radiological Findings:
Pneumonia typically presents as patchy or homogeneous opacities, often localized in one region of the lung. 
Airspace consolidation and infiltrates are common features.

Symptoms:
Patients with pneumonia may experience fever, cough, chest pain, and difficulty breathing.

Treatment:
Treatment usually involves antibiotics, rest, and supportive care. Severe cases may require hospitalization.

Differential Diagnosis:
Conditions such as pleural effusion, atelectasis, or pulmonary edema may show similar radiographic patterns.
