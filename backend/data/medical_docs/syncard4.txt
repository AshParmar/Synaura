Title: Dilated Cardiomyopathy Presenting as Cardiomegaly

Radiological Findings:
- Markedly enlarged cardiac silhouette
- Predominant left ventricular enlargement
- Rounded and enlarged cardiac apex
- Possible pulmonary venous congestion
- Associated pleural effusions in advanced cases

Causes:
- Idiopathic or genetic cardiomyopathy
- Viral myocarditis
- Chronic alcohol use
- Metabolic or systemic disorders

Symptoms:
- Fatigue
- Dyspnea
- Reduced exercise tolerance

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray showing enlarged heart
- Echocardiography showing ventricular dilation and reduced function
- Clinical evaluation for underlying cause

Clinical Importance:
- Indicates impaired cardiac function → risk of heart failure
- Early detection allows medical management
- Important cause of chronic cardiomegaly

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pericardial effusion
- Hypertensive heart disease
- Valvular heart disease
