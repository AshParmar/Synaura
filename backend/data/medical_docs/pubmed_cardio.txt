Title: Cardiomegaly on Chest X-ray Using Cardiothoracic Ratio (CTR)

Radiological Findings:
- Enlarged cardiac silhouette on chest X-ray
- Cardiothoracic ratio (CTR) increased (>0.5 suggestive, >0.55 more specific)
- Globular or widened cardiac borders
- Loss of normal cardiac contour
- Chamber-specific signs:
  - Right atrial enlargement → right heart border bulge
  - Left ventricular enlargement → elongated left heart border
  - Left atrial enlargement → double density or straightening of left border
  - Right ventricular enlargement → elevation of cardiac apex
- Possible displacement of adjacent mediastinal structures

Causes:
- Cardiac chamber enlargement (hypertrophy or dilation)
- Chronic hypertension
- Valvular heart disease
- Cardiomyopathy
- Pericardial effusion (mimics enlargement)

Symptoms:
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Chest discomfort (in some cases)

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray (PA view) for CTR measurement
- Evaluation of cardiac contours for chamber-specific enlargement
- Echocardiography for definitive assessment
- Clinical correlation with cardiac symptoms

Clinical Importance:
- Simple and quick screening tool for heart enlargement
- Helps detect underlying cardiac disease early
- Guides further cardiac evaluation (echo, ECG)
- Important in differentiating true cardiomegaly from mimics

Differential Diagnosis:
- Pericardial effusion (globular heart shape)
- Poor inspiratory effort (false enlargement)
- AP projection X-ray (magnification artifact)
- Mediastinal masses mimicking cardiac enlargement
